Scope

What This Is

This is not another screenwriting syllabus. It is a workbench for The Blindspot: long-form geopolitical, historical, and investigative episodes made by a small team or one stubborn adult in Los Angeles.

The manual starts where production pain starts: the opening seconds, the voice in the edit, the source pile, the visual proof, the sequence, and the final upload. Each live chapter is built to be scanned in thirty seconds, then used during an actual edit.

The source bar is deliberately narrow. Local books and project documents came first. Web sources are limited to official YouTube documentation, YouTube's own engineering blog, Reuters Institute audience research, and primary or operator-grade production commentary.

Key Stat

Reuters Institute's 2025 Digital News Report says 30% of the global sample uses YouTube for news weekly, and social video news consumption rose from 52% in 2020 to 65% in 2025. Translation: The Blindspot is not leaving journalism. It is entering the room where the audience already is.

Naming

Why The Blindspot Almanac

Final pick: The Blindspot Almanac. It names the channel, keeps the field-manual feel, and is broader than edit craft alone. It can hold research, writing, visuals, sound, distribution, and case files without sounding like a course catalog.

Alternatives held: Signal Manual, sharp but too abstract; Cutbook, too edit-only; Show Bible, accurate but too close to the site's Color Bible language.

Steal This

Use "The Blindspot Almanac" in nav, metadata, and cross-links. The subtitle can explain the production scope. The title should sound like part of the channel, not a plugin.

Next

Do Not Add Filler

Phase 2 now ships the remaining method chapters and the first case-file lab. The next expansion should happen only where the source base can support an operational chapter.

Distribution and one-person logistics are still real needs, but they should not become generic creator advice. They need Blindspot-specific operating data, channel tests, and source-safe production notes.
