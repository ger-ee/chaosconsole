The Blindspot / Production Craft / Phase 1

Signal Manual

A randomly entrant field manual for long-form YouTube that wants the rhythm of John Oliver and the spine of Frontline.

5 min read Depth 1 / Field Map Owner: Gary Elbasani
TL;DR
  • Build episodes from promise, proof, payoff.
  • Package before scripting. The title is a draft constraint.
  • Cold opens and VO must survive alone.
  • Use sources like edit decisions, not wallpaper.

Scope

What This Is

This is not another screenwriting syllabus. It is a workbench for The Blindspot: long-form geopolitical, historical, and investigative episodes made by a small team or one stubborn adult in Los Angeles.

The manual starts where production pain starts: the opening seconds, the voice in the edit, the source pile, the visual proof, the sound bed, and the packaging. Each live chapter is built to be scanned in thirty seconds, then used during an actual edit.

The source bar is deliberately narrow. Local books and project documents came first. Web sources are limited to official YouTube documentation, YouTube's own engineering blog, Reuters Institute audience research, and primary or operator-grade production commentary.

Key Stat

Reuters Institute's 2025 Digital News Report says 30% of the global sample uses YouTube for news weekly, and social video news consumption rose from 52% in 2020 to 65% in 2025. Translation: The Blindspot is not leaving journalism. It is entering the room where the audience already is.

Reset

The operating order is simple: hook the viewer, give the voice a job, prove the claim, finish clean.

Naming

Why Signal Manual

Final pick: Signal Manual. It is short, brand-aligned, and less generic than "Production Almanac." The word "signal" fits The Blindspot's premise: extract what matters from noise, propaganda, spectacle, and platform sludge.

Alternatives held: Cutbook, sharper but too edit-only; Show Bible, accurate but already crowded by the site's Color Bible language; Production Almanac, clear but soft.

Steal This

Use the working title in production folders if needed, but put "Signal Manual" in nav. The subtitle can do the explaining. The title should not arrive dragging a suitcase.

Phase 2

Do Not Polish The Map Yet

Phase 1 ships the shell, two real chapters, source ledger, and component documentation. Phase 2 earns expansion only where the sources support operational advice.

The first case files should come after the method chapters, not before. Otherwise they become fan-service autopsies instead of reusable craft patterns.
